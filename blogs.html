Maintenance Tips for Your Car

Keep your car in top shape with these essential maintenance tips and tricks for every vehicle owner.

13 Car Maintenance Tips to Save You Money

Regular maintenance is key to keeping your car running smoothly and avoiding costly repairs. Here are 13 essential tips that can help save you money in the long run by extending the life of your vehicle:

1. Check your spare tire

Keep your backup tire inflated to avoid unexpected surprises when you need it most. Maintenance tip: Test the air pressure of your driving tires and your spare tire once a month.

2. Change the oil

Changing your car’s oil regularly can save you from costly engine repairs. Maintenance tip: Change the oil every 5,000 miles or as per your car manufacturer's recommendations.

3. Keep the battery clean

Corrosion on the battery terminals can cause malfunctions. Keep them clean to avoid battery issues. Maintenance tip: Test your battery twice a year and inspect it for corrosion.

4. Replace the brake pads

Don’t ignore squeaky brakes. Replacing brake pads on time can prevent more expensive repairs. Maintenance tip: Check the brake fluid when you change the oil, and replace the brake system if necessary.

5. Replace your cabin air filter

A clean cabin air filter keeps pollutants out and prevents AC issues. Maintenance tip: Change your cabin air filter every 12 months or 12,000 miles.

6. Get new windshield wipers

Visibility is crucial for safe driving. Replace your windshield wipers when they start to streak or skip. Maintenance tip: Check and replace your windshield wipers at the change of each season.

7. Rotate your tires

Regular tire rotation ensures even wear and extends the life of your tires. Maintenance tip: Rotate your tires every 3,000–5,000 miles.

8. Check the suspension system

Suspension issues can lead to a bumpy ride and other problems. Maintenance tip: Check the shocks and the entire suspension system every 15,000–30,000 miles.

9. Check your coolant

Coolant prevents corrosion and ensures the radiator, water pump, and other systems work efficiently. Maintenance tip: Check your coolant twice a year—once before summer and again before winter.

10. Check your spark plugs

Worn spark plugs can cause engine issues. Replacing them is an inexpensive fix compared to engine overhauls. Maintenance tip: Check and change the spark plugs every 30,000 miles.

11. Inspect belts and hoses

Worn belts and hoses can cause significant damage to your engine. Maintenance tip: Replace your timing belt every 60,000 miles and serpentine belt every 40,000 miles. Replace hoses every four years.

12. Check your lights

Faulty headlights, turn signals, and brake lights can lead to accidents and tickets. Maintenance tip: Check your lights monthly with the help of a friend to ensure they’re functioning correctly.

13. Do the emissions inspection

Keep up with emissions inspections to ensure your car is roadworthy and to avoid fines. Maintenance tip: Take your car for inspection once a year and follow your local regulations.
